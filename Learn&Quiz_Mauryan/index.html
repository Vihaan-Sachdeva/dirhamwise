What affects your credit score?

Payment history is the main factor. Paying bills on time shows you are reliable and helps your score rise.

What does diversification mean?

It means spreading your investments across different types of assets, so one loss does not hurt your whole plan.

What is inflation?

Inflation means prices rise over time. Your money buys less if income does not keep up with that increase.

Gross income versus net income

Gross income is your total earnings before taxes and deductions. Net income is what is left in your account after those costs.

What is APR?

APR is the annual cost of borrowing money. It includes both interest and fees for a loan or credit card.

Why keep an emergency fund?

An emergency fund covers unexpected bills or sudden changes, so you do not need to borrow money at high cost.

What is a stock?

A stock is a share of ownership in a company. Its value can rise or fall depending on how that company performs.

How is a debit card different from credit?

A debit card uses money you already have in your account. A credit card borrows money that you must pay back later.
